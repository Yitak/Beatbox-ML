About Us

Our Team

This company was co-founded by Aaron and Marcus, who have been best friends since childhood. With their combined expertise in music and deep learning, they developed the idea of generating vocal music using Artificial Intelligence (AI), aiming to democratize the use of special vocal techniques in music production.

As a start, they have been constructing a novel generative AI model dedicated specifically for generating vocal percussion (beatboxing). To achieve this, they have personally invested respectable effort in producing an original dataset by collecting suitable audio data themselves. Thanks to these two individuals, the world of music is being pivoted towards a new age.

Many will agree that AI technologies are becoming more mature by the day, impacting the world in more and more ways. However, in the budding field of generative art, people can get quite divided on the ethical concerns: some see AI as a convenient tool, while others may see it as a threat to human creativity. Personally, I see AI-assisted art creation as a great motivator for us humans to express our creative selves in unprecedented ways.

Having had experience developing machine learning projects in the past, I’m so excited to be working on implementing this idea of generative beatboxing, especially since I am a classically trained musician myself - I've picked up so much new musical knowledge while developing this project! I aim to help conceive more interesting and useful projects like this in the future, no matter how broad or niche they may be."

Marcus Leung

Director & Chief Music Consultant

"Hi! I’m Marcus, the Chief Music Consultant. The original dataset mentioned above involves a lot of my own beatboxing vocals. I'm known in professional beatbox circles by my stage name: Mars' Bars. feel free to look me up!
(YouTube search "Mars’ Bars beatbox", Instagram: marsbars_bbx)

Most people think beatboxing is just replicating drum sounds using one’s vocal facilities, but the professional/competitive beatbox scene has evolved so much past that. I would say that contemporary beatboxing is like the Mixed Martial Arts (MMA) of vocal techniques: we learn and practice different styles originating from all over the world and across time, sharing insights with one another along the way, and use these techniques to literally speak music - complete with melodies, harmonies, and rhythms.

I’ve got a large international network of beatboxer friends, and we have all been witnessing a clear rise of interest in the art of beatboxing. People are increasingly recognising and appreciating the vocal techniques practiced by beatboxers in modern music. I would personally love to see what ideas you have that our AI tool can help you with :)"
